# Marketing Platform Options (Campaign Monitor, Klaviyo, Patch)

Last updated: 2026-03-03

Goal: explain why you might use these tools with ROLLER, and what business value they can add.

## Why use any of these platforms?

You use them when you want stronger marketing automation than basic send-outs.

Main business value:

1. Recover lost sales (abandoned carts).
2. Bring guests back more often (winback and birthday journeys).
3. Send more relevant messages (segment by visit, spend, booking behavior).
4. Save staff time (automated journeys instead of manual campaigns).
5. Improve revenue tracking from campaigns.

## Option 1: Campaign Monitor

## What it gives you

1. Native ROLLER integration to push guest segments into Campaign Monitor.
2. Email automation using booking and attendance dates.
3. Uses ROLLER guest fields (name, email, plus optional custom fields like booking value, last visit, total spend).

## Why choose it

1. You want a simpler email-first setup.
2. You want a ROLLER-supported integration path.
3. You do not want API add-on dependency for this integration.

## Business benefit

1. Better timed lifecycle emails (pre-visit, post-visit feedback, missed-visit winback).
2. Better segmentation for membership and birthday campaigns.
3. Lower operational effort for repeat campaigns.

## Key constraints

1. ROLLER notes this integration is Pro+ / certain plans.
2. Primarily email-focused compared with heavier multi-channel stacks.

## Option 2: Klaviyo

## What it gives you

1. Advanced flows for abandoned cart and lifecycle messaging.
2. Strong segmentation and audience targeting.
3. Email + SMS orchestration with compliance guardrails in docs.
4. ROLLER partner integration exists via Smart Segments connector.

## Why choose it

1. You want advanced marketing control and experimentation.
2. You need deeper segmentation and cross-channel journeys.
3. Your team can manage a more complex setup.

## Business benefit

1. Higher conversion from stronger abandoned-cart and re-engagement flows.
2. Better targeting can increase repeat visits and campaign ROI.
3. Useful if growth marketing is a core capability in-house.

## Key constraints

1. ROLLER directory marks this as 3rd-party supported.
2. ROLLER API add-on is required.
3. SMS rules vary by market (for example US cart-SMS rules).

## Option 3: Patch Retention

## What it gives you

1. Attraction-focused retention platform with ROLLER integration.
2. Email + SMS + automated journeys built around venue use cases.
3. One-way sync from ROLLER to Patch for customer/booking data.
4. Onboarding support from Patch team.

## Why choose it

1. You want a venue-specific, faster-to-launch retention program.
2. You want hands-on setup help and prebuilt journeys.
3. You want strong SMS-led campaigns (birthdays, winback, abandoned cart).

## Business benefit

1. Faster campaign activation for franchise parks.
2. Better repeat visit and reactivation potential through attraction-specific journeys.
3. Reduced workload for local teams if central templates are used.

## Key constraints

1. ROLLER API add-on is required.
2. Integration is owned by Patch (3rd-party), not ROLLER-native.
3. Claims like retention uplift are vendor claims and should be validated in pilot.

## Practical decision guide

1. Choose **Campaign Monitor** if you want the simplest ROLLER-supported email automation path.
2. Choose **Klaviyo** if you want advanced, data-heavy lifecycle marketing and have team capacity.
3. Choose **Patch** if you want attraction-specific retention with faster guided rollout.

## Suggested rollout path

1. Start with one pilot market and one KPI set:
   - abandoned-cart recovery rate
   - repeat visit rate (30/60/90 day)
   - campaign-attributed revenue
2. Run A/B comparison for 6-8 weeks.
3. Scale the winner to more parks.
